About

I am a PhD student in the Genomics and Evolutionary Dynamics team supervised by Professor Trevor Graham at the Institute of Cancer Research. My project concerns the evolution of cancer metastasis.

Prior to starting my PhD, I completed an MBiochem at the University of Oxford, carrying out my Master's research project in the Oxford Protein Informatics Group.

My scientific interests are broad, including the role of non-coding RNA, ML approaches to biology, and cancer evolution. Ultimately, my goal is to leverage computational power for deciphering the increasingly vast troves of available data in order to understand biological systems and how they break down in cancer.
